2007 · Adam Sutcliffe · Urbanization and City Planning

The European City and Green Space: London, Stockholm, Helsinki and St Petersburg, 1850-2000

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

Green space was on the agenda when the history of urban and regional planning became an organised field in the 1970s, but little has been done except in America. A promising monograph was commissioned in 1993 but nothing has come of it. This collective volume is therefore to be welcomed. It is the product of two teams, one composed of Nordic and Russian historians centred on Helsinki and the other of English historians connected with London. Planning has taken over from park design and these essays are mainly ‘planning history’ as it is now known. Social history is present but mainly in the pre-planning era of the nineteenth century. The diffuse study method means that each essay stands on its own. The threadbare preface by the series editors does not help; but the editor's introduction, jointly authored with a Baltic planner, lacks nothing in ambition, as open space is presented...
