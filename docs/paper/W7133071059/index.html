Preservation of former military fortresses: Experience of the Suomenlinna Sea Fortress and prospects for the restoration of Tarakaniv Fort

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

Tarakaniv Fort (or Dubensky Fort), built in 1873-1890, is a defensive structure located near the village of Tarakaniv, Dubensky District, Rivne Region, Ukraine. The fort is quite distinguished by its carefully thought-out defensive solutions, equipped with a large number of underground passages and combat posts, which are still unexplored to this day. It became an example of the latest engineering thought of its time. Due to its uniqueness, Tarakaniv Fort was granted the status of an architectural monument of the 19th century. However, unfortunately, today the fort is in a rather poor condition, almost on the verge of complete destruction. The question of the need for its urgent restoration and preservation has remained open for several decades now. Suomenlinna (or Sveaborg) is a bastion system of fortifications located on a group of islands near Helsinki, Finland. Built in the second half of the 18th century, the fortress is a unique example of European military engineering thought of its time and is included in the list of UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Today, Suomenlinna is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Finland. It has managed not only to preserve its historical heritage and unique natural landscapes, but also to create a living community. The purpose of this work is to initially investigate the history of the development of both fortress complexes. Subsequently, to analyze the principles and approaches used in the preservation of the Suomenlinna site and adapt them to the context of the Tarakaniv Fort. The final result of the thesis is a conceptual design project for the restoration and development of the Tarakaniv Fort.
