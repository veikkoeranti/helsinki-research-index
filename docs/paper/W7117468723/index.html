Urban toponymic detachment. Place names as symbols of urban change

OpenAlex · DOI

Abstract

The study explores the concepts of toponymic attachment and toponymic detachment in urban environments, focusing on how naming practices reflect place detachment amid urban change. It highlights the role of toponymic detachment as a means of addressing dissonant pasts. Through interviews with residents of the Vuosaari neighbourhood, Helsinki, the study examines how people perceive and use place names, both official and unofficial, and how these practices relate to their sense of place and identity. By examining two specific examples of naming practices in Vuosaari, the article illustrates and makes visible the politics of naming. The research utilizes folk onomastics to analyse discussions about names, emphasizing the dialogic relationship between people and places.
