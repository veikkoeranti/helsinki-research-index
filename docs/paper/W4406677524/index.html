Burning The Bull

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

Tamara Searle is an independent director and performance-maker based in Australia, where she is an Artistic Associate of Back to Back Theatre. A graduate of the Australian Ballet School, Searle holds an MA in performance practice from Monash University, and has been an actor, dancer, director, choreographer, and dramaturg for live and digital performance in Australia, the UK, Germany, and elsewhere. This article, based on observations made during a 2018 residency at the Helsinki International Artist Programme (HIAP), details the annual Viaporin Kekri ceremony on the island of Suomenlinna, a UNESCO World Heritage site off the coast of Finland. As a contemporary revival of an ancient harvest ritual, the Kekri festival offers Searle the opportunity to explore how a ritual performance based in nature can take on new and different meanings for participants in a world marked by industrialization and climate change.
