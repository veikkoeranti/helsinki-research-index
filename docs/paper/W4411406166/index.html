Etnisten vähemmistöjen asumisvalintojen eriytyminen

OpenAlex · DOI · fi

Abstract

This article examines the housing market agency of working-age Chinese, Vietnamese, and Somali speakers in the Helsinki metropolitan area, using interview and statistical data. The theoretical framework highlights the role of housing supply and institutional factors, and differences in economic, social, and cultural capital, with a focus on the concept of the property mind (ownership mentality), which influences tenure choices and housing pathways. Findings show distinct qualities in the analysed groups' housing market agency: Chinese participants perceive the Finnish housing regime favouring homeownership and real estate investment, with some owning multiple properties. Vietnamese and Somali speaking young adults are less investment-oriented. Institutional constraints are limiting homeownership among Somali-background individuals, some of whom see right of occupancy as a way to advance one's position in the housing market.
