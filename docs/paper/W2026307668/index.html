1984 · Seppo T. Rinne · Housing Market and Economics

The Development of Housing Attribute Prices in the Helsinki Metropolitan Area

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

The development of the implicit price structure of privately financed old multi‐storey house dwellings is studied by comparing relative hedonic prices at two points in time and in two parts of the Helsinki metropolitan area. The housing characteristics, whose relative prices have risen most, are found to be the small number of rooms, effectiveness in use of floor space, quality and “status” of neighbourhood and quality of dwellings. With the growth of price differences, the housing market is polarizing socially and geographically. This seems to be due mainly to the growth of the area, a shift in demand from satisfying quantitative housing needs to improving housing quality and investing, and the diminishing supply of rented dwellings.
