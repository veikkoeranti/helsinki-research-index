Social well-being in urban housing and implications for adaptability and flexibility strategies.

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

Increased demand for urban housing driven by urbanisation resulted majority of residents in urban areas of Finland living in apartment housing. With technical qualities such as energy efficiency and material standards of newly constructed apartments remaining high, concerns are being raised about the quality of housing with regard to human well-being. The social well-being in urban housing remains under-explored compared to the other two well-being aspects considered as physical well-being and mental well-being. This thesis investigates social well-being in urban housing and implications for adaptability and flexibility strategies through its research process. The research consists of a literature review followed by a case study analysis of related residential building schemes. The literature review of the thesis focuses on identifying the influencing factors of social well-being in urban housing. Key influencing factors are identified as: Sense of Community, Perceived Safety, Privacy and Personal Space, Inclusive Housing, Versatile Housing and Affordability of Housing. Adaptability and flexibility are identified as essential components for quality housing which also related to versatile and inclusive housing and promoting social sustainability. Then the literature review followed with a detailed discussion on adaptability and flexibility. Three case study projects were selected based on their different approaches to social well-being and implications for adaptability and flexibility. Each project was analysed in three different scales classified into apartment unit scale, building scale and neighbourhood scale. Characteristics of the projects were identified and suggestions and improvements were developed based on the research findings. The findings of the research suggest that the key influences identified in the thesis significantly contribute to social well-being in housing. Specifically concerning urban housing, it can be identified that adaptable and flexible design solutions are essential to address the strategies for social well-being as there are greater spatial limitations and diverse needs of residents in urban contexts. The thesis suggests that the adaptability and flexibility measures to be incorporated without reducing the other important housing design quality factors and recommends further research on adaptability and flexibility in different dimensions.
