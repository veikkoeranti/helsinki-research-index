Arrests for Drunkenness - Two Capital Cities Compared

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

In Helsinki in recent years the rate of arrests for drunkenness has been from seven to thirteen times as high as in Copenhagen. The object of the present study is to elucidate the values and norms of the formal social control systems, and to chart the factors bearing upon the difference between the cities. The factors that markedly contribute to the difference are the criteria for arrest and the access to places that serve as shelters against police action. The statistics relating to arrests for drunkenness in the different Scandinavian countries cannot be used merely on the basis of their ‘verbal content’ - attention should also be given to their factual content, that is ‘institutional differences’.
