The renewal of the hospital area in Koskela - A battle between preservation and urban infill

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

To preserve or to replace - to improve or to leave to deteriorate? \nRenewal of the hospital area in Koskela can be viewed as a battle between preservation and urban infill. \nThe objective of urban renewal is to develop the area but the means to achieve the objective polarize opinions. \n\nThe hospital area was originally built for alms-house and workhouse purposes during the second decade of the 20th century and carries historical value. \nThe hospital area as a whole is a representative example of the ideology behind health care institution building of its time. \nThe architectural value of the area is due to the old buildings themselves and their arrangement, and the views and the green areas that support the cohesive whole. \nWhen the area is developed, the preservation of the unity of the hospital area needs to be taken into consideration. \n\nThe loosely built park area and the old hospital maintenance yards attract infill building. \nThe development area is situated on the edge of the downtown area with good transport connections to the city center. \nThe surrounding housing areas are among the most popular housing areas in Helsinki. \nIt is easy to justify infill building. \nHowever, the starting point for the development has to be a survey of the buildings and surroundings and the recognition of the architectural and historical values. \nThe most important characteristics of the area need to be identified before it is possible to plan more construction. \n\nThe development should be based on the objectives of functionality and vitality of the area. \nBesides preservation, enough space for infill buildings must be found. \nIf the goals of both preservation and Infill are not observed, urban renewal won't be successful. \nA lack of dialogue makes the reconciliation between conflicting interests difficult. \n\nIt is possible to highlight and emphasize the best qualities and characteristics of the existing buildings and surroundings by means of infill building. \nUrban renewal brings new residents, activities and vibrancy to the area. \nSimultaneously it reduces the pressure to substantially alter the existing buildings. \n\nThe most serious threat to the development of the area is the lack of common vision. \nShort-sighted solutions jeopardize the preservation of the old as well as the possibilities of new construction and threaten the comprehensive development of the area.
