Nordic Cities and GHG Emissions Reduction : Aligning with the UNFCCC COP29 Mitigation Work Programme for Climate-Resilient Urban Development

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

The Nordic region is leading in urban decarbonization through spatial planning, electrification, carbon storage, and circular economy models. Key findings highlight innovative approaches like smart mobility hubs in Bergen, emission-free construction in Oslo, and the use of bio-based materials in Denmark. Despite challenges, the region is advancing low-carbon development with renewable energy systems, green-blue infrastructure, and circular business models. Collaboration, policy, and financial support remain critical to scaling these solutions for sustainable urban growth. The report also emphasizes the role of digital tools in boosting energy efficiency, as seen in Helsinki's Kalasatama district, and highlights successful public-private partnerships like CO-PI in Copenhagen. However, challenges remain in retrofitting, regulatory barriers, and financing. #NordicInnovation #NetZero #CircularEconomy #UrbanDevelopment #Sustainability
