The Helsinki suburbs of Tapiola and Vantaanpuisto: post‐war planning by the architect Aarne Ervi

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

Aarne Ervi (1910–77) was one of the most active and versatile Finnish architects of the decades following the Second World War. Despite the fact that Ervi built a reputation for himself as a designing architect, this study highlights his activity and importance as an urban planner. This paper examines the co‐operation between architect Aarne Ervi and two public utility housing organizations in Finland: Asuntosäätiö, the ‘Housing Foundation’ and Asuntosäästäjät ry, the ‘Home Savers Society’. This co‐operation included planning and building the suburban housing areas of Tapiola and Vantaanpuisto in the Helsinki metropolitan region during the 1950s and 1960s. These two organizations and their policies are compared. Ervi’s planning is analysed and the reasons for the implementation of these projects and the location of the housing areas interpreted. A number of topics are considered: modern planning in Finland, the growth of the Helsinki metropolitan area, public utility organizations and suburban projects, and Ervi’s modern planning.
