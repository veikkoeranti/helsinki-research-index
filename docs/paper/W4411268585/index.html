A Long Cycle of Expansive Learning in a Food Cooperative: A Multi-Lens Analysis

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

This study presents a longitudinal, multi-lens analysis of expansive learning within the Herttoniemi Food Cooperative, a social movement focused on sustainable food production located in Helsinki.We employ the theory of expansive learning to analyze a year-long process of collective learning among the cooperative's board members.The aim of this study is to understand the innovative learning process of the board as they faced the challenge of financial viability.The data of this study consist of recordings from 26 board meetings and a planning day.Our findings show that the concept of degrowth acted as a central integrative factor for developing tangible solutions.The starting point of innovations was real-world problems that participants encountered in the board meetings as they were trying to solve the complex object of financial sustainability.An intervention planning day was needed to refine new tools in their mature form to be implemented later successfully.
