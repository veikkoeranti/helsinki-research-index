Challenges in Identifying the Child's Experiences and Assessing Adverse Consequences of Stalking from the Child's Perspective

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

In this chapter we describe the challenges of identifying and assessing the child's experiences and the adverse consequences of stalking as reflected in our own experiences of working with clients at the Finnish National Stalking Center (Tukikeskus Varjo). In the case of long-term exposure to stalking, it is essential that the network of professionals engage in ongoing assessment of the consequences of stalking and the child's needs. Close collaboration between professionals and the child, the child's safe parent, and the other professionals working with the child's family is paramount to understanding the child's perspective in each situation. In our work, we have observed that to develop an understanding of the child's position in and experiences of ex-partner stalking, professionals must be able to identify the multiple challenges involved and be prepared to respond to them. These challenges are related to society's operational environment and the service system.
