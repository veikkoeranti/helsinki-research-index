2020 · Markus Pesonen · Housing Market and Economics

The impact of operating costs on housing prices in the Helsinki metropolitan area

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

The purpose of the thesis is to study how the differences in maintenance charges (€/sqm/month) impact the housing prices in the Helsinki metropolitan area. The thesis presents general housing market pricing theories as well as some behavioral biases which could lead to mispricing in the housing market. In the empirical part, a dataset containing approximately 120,000 apartment transactions is used to estimate a hedonic pricing model using linear regression.&#13;\n&#13;\nThe data used in the thesis is received from Kiinteistönvälitysalan Keskusliitto ry, KVKL HSP- hintaseurantapalvelu. The used data covers individual apartment transactions in the Helsinki metropolitan area from 2010 onwards. The models were estimated on the whole sample, age limited sample and on a sample containing only apartments located on freehold plots.&#13;\n&#13;\nThe outcomes from the analyses differ a lot depending on the used dataset. The findings might suggest that an increase in maintenance charges has a negative impact on the price per sqm of the apartment. In addition, the outcomes indicate that the magnitude of the impact is higher when the increase in maintenance charge is caused by the apartment being located on a leasehold plot. Other maintenance charge components might have notably lower impact on apartment prices.&#13;\n&#13;\nHowever, the use of a relatively simple model and characteristics which are undiscoverable from the data, make the model vulnerable to omitted variable biases, decreasing the reliability of the study.
