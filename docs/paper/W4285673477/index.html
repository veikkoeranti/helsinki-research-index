Re-Evaluating Crisis Politics in the Work of Aku Louhimies

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

This chapter unpacks the thematic and ideological representations of crisis in Louhimies’s feature films <italic>Paha maa</italic> (<italic>Frozen Land</italic>, 2005) and <italic>Vuosaari</italic> (<italic>Naked Harbour</italic>, 2012). Both explore contemporary life in Helsinki through a patchwork of interconnected and parallel narratives where the characters’ lives are marred by domestic violence, alcoholism, unemployment and social alienation. However, despite the clear contrasts between dominant historical conceptualisations of social unity in Finland and the stark realities represented in these films, I argue that Louhimies appears to reappropriate the theme of continuity. I claim that the central tension in these films revolves around whether they ask us to view crisis as an external affliction of an underlying and established social order, or whether they suggest that crisis is, in fact, a fundamental component of the Finnish social model itself. We can use this debate to explore the depth and meaning of Louhimies’s relationship with the contemporary political landscape.
