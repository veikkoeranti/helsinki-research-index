1970 · Maija Kärki · Cultural Heritage Management and Preservation

From a modern power plant to a factory of culture

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

Meanings and values at the Hanasaari A power plant as an object of industrial heritage in Helsinki 1957–2007.The Hanasaari A power plant in Helsinki in Finland has gone through several fundamental changes during its life cycle. It was constructed at the turn of the 1960s to produce energy in a modern, efficient manner and delivered both electricity and heat to Helsinki for several decades.After being converted to a reserve power plant in the beginning of the 21st century, it served as a “cultural factory” where music videos and television series were shot. After the 2007 decision to demolish the power plant, Helsinki Energy launched a documentation project aimed at preserving the history and industrial heritage of the power plant. This article describes the multi-faceted life cycle of Hanasaari A and examines the underlying values and meanings associated with its industrial heritage.
