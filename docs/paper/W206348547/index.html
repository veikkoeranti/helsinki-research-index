Slang Toponyms in Early Twentieth Century Helsinki

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

In this article, we analyse spatial slang coinages by boys and young men from Sörnäinen, a bilingual&#13;\nworking-class neighbourhood in early twentieth century Helsinki (1900–1939). During this period, Helsinki&#13;\nslang developed into a unique slang spoken among the working class of both Finnish and Swedish language&#13;\nbackgrounds in their densely occupied quarters. In their childhood, male juveniles of Sörnäinen used many&#13;\nplace-referring slang words in both appellative (classifying) and proprial (identifying) functions. When the&#13;\nsame males got older and began to move more widely in different kinds of social settings across Helsinki,&#13;\nthe use of these kinds of double-functional nouns decreased in favour of slang names derived from the&#13;\ncity’s official nomenclature. The primary research material of the article covers a set of interviews made&#13;\nbetween the 1920s and the 1980s.
