”It is a long process and it will take time but it does not mean that it is impossible – it is possible!” : experiences of workers from three projects in prevention of honour-related violence among patriarchal immigrant communities in Finland

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

In this study the focus is on examining the ways and methods to prevent honour-related&#13;\nviolence among patriarchal immigrant communities. The phenomenon is proved to&#13;\nappear in Finland and hence it is important to find preventative solution-centred tools&#13;\nfor it. The study also aims at addressing the challenges concerning the work as well as&#13;\nfinding the developing points for future work in Finland. The data was collected from&#13;\nthe main workers of three projects which focus on their work on prevention of HRV.&#13;\nThe research is qualitative and semi-structured thematic interviews were used to collect&#13;\nthe data.&#13;\n&#13;\nOne of the key findings of the study is the use of dialogue, whether using some&#13;\nfunctional method or a more traditional method. Through the dialogue and knowledge&#13;\nincrease the thought patterns can be challenged as alternative ways to think and act can&#13;\nbe offered. These are hoped to be activated in mind when some honour issue appears.&#13;\nThe study results speak for an indirect way of addressing the issue among the target&#13;\ngroup so that people would not be driven away. Cultural sensitivity and gender&#13;\nsensitivity seem to be good approaches in work. It is highlighted that the real change&#13;\nwill only start from inside of communities which should be kept in mind at work.&#13;\n&#13;\nCertain challenges stand out in results like unreachability of some people, the&#13;\nuncertainty of the work, integration problems as well as power and position changes&#13;\ninside families. These challenges point out also how the work could be developed in&#13;\nfuture like establishing more channels for reaching people and strengthening the&#13;\nintegration process of different family members. The awareness and training for&#13;\nprofessionals and impact on the authorities to be able to develop the work is strongly&#13;\nrecommended.
