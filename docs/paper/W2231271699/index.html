The Environmental Design Parameters AffectingThe New Lay-out Of The Vuosaari Small CraftMarina

OpenAlex · DOI · en

Abstract

The Eastern part of Helsinki is growing fast. New urban areas have been designed both inland and on-shore of this sub-urban zone, called Vuosaari. It forms an interesting area for city planning due to demands of the new infrastructure, natural shore-lines with protected natural areas and the close situation of the new Port of Helsinki. Thus when planning a new small craft marina a lot of design parameters were affected the final design, however, environmental protection issues had the greatest impact. This paper describes a marina design concept for 1000 boats and yachts in connection with the city planning phase. The design work had to take into account the existence of a sensitive conservation area in the middle of the proposed marina area, which totally changed the design philosophy from the initial ideas and preliminary lay-outs. The main goal of the work was to spare the sensitive narrow shore-line, create enough protective space around the area and to direct activities more offshore than onshore. An environmental impact assessment (EIA) type of evaluation was thus an important and essential part of the marina design. Main design parameters, such as the wave field, ice problems as well as the planning principles are discussed. One important feature of the work was to evaluate the water exchange and the quality of the sea water of the marina.
