Second Housing in Finland - Perspective of Mobility

OpenAlex · en

Abstract

Second housing in Finland is a large phenomenon with deep historical and cultural roots. In this paper the issue of second housing is approached from the perspectives of mobility and urban-rural interaction. At first a brief statistical overview on Finnish second housing is summarized. The term second home is used as a general notion for rural recreational residences. There are over 465.000 second homes in Finland and every sixth household owns one. However, it is estimated that every second family has an access to some second home. In addition second housing is here discussed as a way of life from the point of view of changing societal trends. The rise in income level, the development of communication and information technology, as well as increase of leisure time and individual time-place flexibility are examples of the trends affecting second housing. The main focus of the paper is on the preliminary analysis of a survey which was conducted in June 2004. The questionnaires were sent to 2500 persons who are living in the capital region of Helsinki and possess a second home in the Finnish Lakeland. The survey questions related to physical and virtual mobility are highlighted. Topics related to physical mobility and travel experiences are examined. Issues concerning virtual mobility and remote work are discussed as well. The analysis of the data is done bearing in mind the environmentally and socially sustainable future of second housing.
